  • Campus

    Co-Founded Design for America

    In December 2013, a small small group of MSU students gathered together with a single vision- to unite students accross disciplines in order to create social impact in our community. After a year long application, we gained acceptance into a competitive national network of student social entrepeneurs.

  • Community

    Leading Project Innovation

    Each semester, DFA uses empathy to immerse themselves in unfamiliar problem spaces and develop a solution with and for the user.

    Spring 2014: Food Insecurity Developed a nutrition program in a local elementary school encouraging students to eat their fruits and vegetables to reduce food insecurity.

    Spring 2015: Cerebral Palsy Worked with assistive technologies and those with cerebral palsy to innovate around the idea of bettering the experience of walker users.

    Spring 2016: Spare Change Implemented Jarduino- a netowrk of smart giving jars, connecting local businesses to local charities by utilizing spare change-into our community.

  • National

    Sustaining Social Impact

    Since founding DFA, I have developed frameworks for maximizing our social impact: events inolving our community and strengthening our teams, an internal and external leadership structure, and a Midsemester Meetup in which DFA MSU hosted 5 surrounding schools for an Innovation Design Weekend. DFA now partners with MSU's learning centers and entrepeneurship department.

    In August 2015, I became an Intercultural Aide, crossing differencing and building community on campus.   • Resume

Educational Advertisement

I developed this ad to localize a discussion I was to lead on cultural appropriation.

As an Intercultural Aide I lead weekly discussions on campus addressing relevant social issues. I designed this ad to both attract people to our discussion and educate those who couldn't make it on a trending topic during the time. My goal was to highlight the historical context against a modern issue of controversy.

Poster Design

I designed this ad to help students rethink the classic use of a woman's body in advertising.

As an Intercultural Aide I lead weekly discussions on campus addressing relevant social issues. This particular discussion was to touch upon sex, society and the entertainment industry. I sought to design a poster that captured the grittiness of the issue, placing in context the overly-used woman's body in advertising as a mere money-making tool of the industry.

Job Advertisment

I infused the history of two programs dating to the 1960s and 90s to detail a single job description.

Developing this advertisement took research and listening to the histories of programs rooted in activism and bridging racial gaps through social action, relationship-building, discussion groups, and service. I designed four versions of the final ad featuring students representing all cultures and backgrounds within the program.

Infographic

I captured a 2-day, 9-hour design workshop through illustrating our process.

At the close of a 2 day design weekend, I sat down to begin an email reiterating key vocabulary and felt weighed down by the ineffectiveness of a wordy email. I set out to capture the core concepts instead, in an illustrated process guide.

Wireframe

Wireframes were one step to increasing usability of the MSU Recycling site.

My project team was challenged with increasing the usability of the MSU Recycling Center's online presense. Through data collection and wireframing, we were able to deliver a tangible action-plan to our clients.
