Educational Advertisement



GOAL

Raise awareness by highlighting the historical context behind a modern issue of controversy.

PROCESS

Historical and cultural research into where the issue was talked about and addressed.

TOOLS

Adobe Illustrator

As an Intercultural Aide I lead weekly discussions on campus addressing relevant social issues. I designed this ad to both attract people to our discussion and educate those who couldn't make it on a trending topic during the time. My goal was to highlight the historical context against a modern issue of controversy.

Poster Design



GOAL

Increase thinking behind the use of womans' bodies in advertising and media.

PROCESS

Stetches, media overlays, and multiple iterations.

TOOLS

Adobe Illustrator

As an Intercultural Aide I lead weekly discussions on campus addressing relevant social issues. This particular discussion was to touch upon sex, society and the entertainment industry. I sought to design a poster that captured the grittiness of the issue, placing in context the overly-used woman's body in advertising as a mere money-making tool of the industry.

Job Advertisement



GOAL

Infuse the history of two programs dating to the 1960s and 90s to detail a single job description.

PROCESS

In depth research and interviews with program leaders and students.

TOOLS

Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Photography

Developing this advertisement took research and listening to the histories of programs rooted in activism and bridging racial gaps through social action, relationship-building, discussion groups, and service. I designed four versions of the final ad featuring students representing all cultures and backgrounds within the program.

Infographic

I captured a 2-day, 9-hour design workshop through illustrating our process.

At the close of a 2 day design weekend, I sat down to begin an email reiterating key vocabulary and felt weighed down by the ineffectiveness of a wordy email. I set out to capture the core concepts instead, in an illustrated process guide.

Wireframe

Wireframes were one step to increasing usability of the MSU Recycling site.

My project team was challenged with increasing the usability of the MSU Recycling Center's online presense. Through data collection and wireframing, we were able to deliver a tangible action-plan to our clients.

Promotional Ad

My targeted audience for this ad was open-minded students seeking to create social change across disciplines.

I included our design process as well as illustrations of our projects to attract students by interest of project and/or attraction to the processs.

System Design

I chose a system of objects with personal signifance and united them through design principles.

I sought to create an essense of movement upon the stationary objects through shadow, and a feeling of invitation from the afternoon glow.

Animal Icon

I used minimalism to create an iconic representation of an animal for a graphic design project.

Through over 50 sketches of monkeys, I developed a shape that captured the free-form and flexibility of a spider monkey.

Logo Design

I infused the curriculum of "bird teams" as illustrations of support into the logo design.

After over 30 sketches I designed type that captured key themes of the MRULE/ICA program: boldness and interconnection, while infusing the classic bird logo.

Packaging

I used UX methods to understand my user and problem spaces, as well as an understanding of sustainability principles to develop an efficient and useful packaging design.

I began with thinking about different materials and chose to go with the most eco-friendly and recycable. I also dedicated time to developing something compact and usable at home and on the go.

Collaborative Space

I used an understanding of the 4 pleasures to design and create a usable work room for the Intercultural Aide program.

I designed a room that would function as a space for the ICA's to stay informed, get to relax and develop relationships with other ICAs, and also work on both individual and collaboritive projects.

Product Development

I worked on a project team developing a way for our community to utilize spare change.

Our team was split into the technological developers of the wifi-infused product, and the service designers. I was part of the service design team. We spent the semester doing user testing for our products size and decor and developing an efficient system for use.

Interaction Display

I prototyped an interactive display for the MSU Kresge Art Center.

App Prototype

I prototyped a functional app serving the student body on a college campus.

I used invision to create a final, workable version of the application.

Service Design

I worked on an investigative research team aimed at understanding the functionality of the non-profit organization Meals and Wheels.

Innovation Model

After two years of working on innovation project teams, I developed a model to guide our work.

I categorized the 3 major forms of insight we needed to have when innovating solutions to problems and worked with my studio to develop questions in each of these areas.

Informational Booklet

I innovated around the best form of displaying information in a way the user could understand a complex system of information.

My final booklet walked the user through a journey of Che Guevara's trip through South America- allowing them to engage in his journey through sketches of his experiences and personal journal entries Che wrote during his time.

Leadership Model

I used a combination of human-centered design and systemic design to develop a new leadership structure with my studio.

Our process began with listening to current voices to identify problems in the studio. We then used systemic design to analyze how the problems were connected and made core changes in the leadership structure that would directly target the original problems.
